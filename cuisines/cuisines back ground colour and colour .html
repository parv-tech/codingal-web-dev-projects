Naan

Naan is a popular type of leavened flatbread that originates from South Asia, particularly India, though it has roots in Persian cuisine. Traditionally baked in a tandoor (a cylindrical clay oven), naan is known for its soft, pillowy texture with slightly crispy, charred edges. It's typically made from white flour, yeast, salt, and yogurt or milk, which gives it a distinct tang and tenderness. Naan is often served hot and brushed with ghee or butter, and it may be topped with garlic, herbs, or stuffed with ingredients like cheese or minced meat. While naan is a staple in Indian and Pakistani cuisines, it has become popular globally, commonly served with curries, lentils, and grilled meats.

Navrattan korma

Navrattan Korma is a rich and flavorful Indian curry known for its creamy texture and colorful assortment of ingredients. The name Navrattan means "nine gems," referring to the traditional inclusion of nine different vegetables, fruits, or nuts in the dish. Typically made with a mix of vegetables like carrots, peas, potatoes, and bell peppers, it also includes sweet elements such as pineapple or raisins, along with cashews or almonds for crunch. The base is a mildly spiced, creamy sauce made from yogurt, cream, or coconut milk, often thickened with ground nuts. Navrattan Korma is a Mughlai-inspired dish, showcasing the fusion of savory and sweet flavors. It is usually served with naan or basmati rice and is especially popular at festive or celebratory meals.

Pakhala

Pakhala is a traditional fermented rice dish from the eastern Indian state of Odisha, and it's especially popular in neighboring regions like West Bengal, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, and Assam. Made by soaking cooked rice in water, often overnight, pakhala is typically consumed cold and may be seasoned with salt and sometimes curd (yogurt), green chilies, or mustard seeds. The soaking process allows slight fermentation, which imparts a tangy flavor and aids in digestion. It is often served with simple side dishes like fried or mashed vegetables, green chilies, or fish fry. Pakhala is especially popular during the hot summer months as it cools the body and helps prevent heatstroke. In Odisha, March 20 is even celebrated as Pakhala Dibasa (Pakhala Day), honoring this humble, nourishing dish.
